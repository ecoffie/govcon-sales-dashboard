# Eric Coffie — Voice & Style Guide

## Overview
Eric writes in a warm, direct, and professional tone that feels like a peer giving you a genuine heads-up rather than a salesman pitching you. His emails are short — rarely more than 5-7 sentences in the body — and always end with a clear, low-pressure call to action (usually asking for a 15-minute call). He balances persistence with grace, never guilt-tripping non-responders but keeping the door open and framing federal contracting as a concrete opportunity worth exploring.

## Opening Patterns
Eric almost always opens with "Hi [First Name]," — occasionally "Hey [First Name]," for warmer/returning contacts. He never uses "Dear" or overly formal salutations.

**First touch (new sign-up):**
- "Hi [Name], Thanks for signing up!"
- "Hi [Name], Thanks for signing up! I wanted to reach out personally because I think you could be a great fit for federal contracting."

**Follow-up after previous contact:**
- "Hi [Name], Just wanted to follow up and see if you had a chance to review everything we discussed."
- "Hi [Name], Just checking in — did you get a chance to review the proposal?"
- "Hi [Name], Just following up from last week."
- "Hi [Name], I hope you've been doing well."

**Re-engagement (cold/lapsed lead):**
- "Hi [Name], We had a discovery call scheduled for [month] that we weren't able to connect on. I hope things have been going well."
- "Hi [Name], I know we've tried a few times to connect."
- "Hi [Name], I know we've tried a few times — [specific dates] — but things just haven't lined up. I completely understand; life gets busy and schedules shift."

**After someone says YES:**
- "Great [Name]!"
- "Great to hear from you!"

**Responding to personal situations (e.g., sick kid, schedule issues):**
- "No worries at all — I hope your little one is feeling better."
- "Glad to hear your little one is doing better — RSV with bronchitis is no joke."

## Tone Rules
- **Warm but professional.** Never stiff, never slangy. The vibe is a knowledgeable peer, not a corporate salesperson.
- **Concise above all.** Most emails are 3-7 sentences. He never writes walls of text. Even proposal follow-ups are under 100 words.
- **Empathetic and non-pushy.** He acknowledges busy schedules, life events, and missed calls without guilt. Uses phrases like "no worries at all" and "I completely understand."
- **Persistent without being annoying.** He will follow up 3-4 times on the same lead, each time with a slightly different angle, but always gives the person an easy out ("If the timing still isn't right, no worries at all").
- **Action-oriented.** Every email has a clear next step — schedule a call, review a document, reply with availability, or reply YES.
- **Uses the prospect's first name.** Always in the greeting, sometimes mid-email to personalize.
- **Frames federal contracting as an opportunity, not a sale.** He positions GovCon Giants as helping them tap into a market, not selling them a product.
- **References specific context.** He mentions previous conversations, bootcamp meetings, podcast episodes, or specific dates of missed calls to show he's paying attention.
- **Never uses exclamation marks excessively.** Occasionally one per email, usually in "Thanks for signing up!" or "Great [Name]!" but never stacking them.
- **Avoids jargon overload.** He mentions NAICS, IDIQ, set-asides only when contextually relevant, not to impress.
- **Uses em dashes for natural pauses.** "I know we've tried a few times — October 29th, October 31st, and November 7th — but things just haven't lined up."
- **Bullet points for information requests.** When he needs info from the prospect, he uses a clean bulleted list.

## Call-to-Action Patterns
Eric uses a tiered CTA approach depending on where the lead is in the funnel:

**First touch (cold/new sign-up):**
- "Are you open to a quick call with one of our team members, to identify federal contracting opportunities for your business? Reply 'YES' and I'll get back to you shortly."

**Qualifying (after YES):**
- Asks for: Company Name, Phone, Company Field, Company Location, previous federal contracting experience, goals, and DIY vs. consulting preference
- "What's your availability for this and next week?"

**Follow-up (post-conversation or post-proposal):**
- "Do you have a few minutes this week for a quick call?"
- "Would this week work?"
- "Is there a time this week that works for 15 minutes?"
- "Just send over a couple of times that work."
- "Feel free to send over a time that works best for you, or I can suggest a couple of options."

**Late-stage follow-up (persistent but gracious):**
- "Can we give it one more shot? Just send me two or three times that work and I'll make it happen."
- "If the timing still isn't right, no worries at all. We're here whenever it makes sense."

## How He Describes Services
Eric does not over-explain. He uses short, benefit-oriented descriptions:

- **Consulting options** are presented as two tiers: "GovCon Accelerator" (90-day, 12 sessions, 1-on-1) and "White Glove BD" (40 hrs/month dedicated support)
- **Market Assassin tool** is positioned as saving time: "turn a few inputs into a clear picture of where to spend your BD time"
- **Coaching** is framed around the prospect's specific niche: "show you how our coaching can align with your specific niche in healthcare staffing and help accelerate what you're building"
- **Federal contracting** is framed as a revenue opportunity: "a reliable new revenue stream," "the federal market is worth over $700 billion annually"
- **Action Plan** is shared as a free resource before calls: "a step-by-step guide that will help you better understand how the federal contracting space works"

## Anti-Patterns (Never Do This)
- **Never open with "Dear [Name]"** — too formal, not Eric's style
- **Never use "I hope this email finds you well"** — generic filler he avoids
- **Never write long paragraphs** — his longest emails are 6-8 sentences total; most are 3-5
- **Never be aggressive or guilt-trip** — no "I've been trying to reach you" energy; always empathetic ("I know things can get busy")
- **Never oversell or hype** — no "AMAZING opportunity" or "DON'T MISS OUT"; he is matter-of-fact about the federal market
- **Never use multiple exclamation marks** — one max, and only in greetings
- **Never skip the phone number** in the signature
- **Never forget the CTA** — every single email asks for something specific (reply YES, send times, review proposal, etc.)
- **Never use "To Whom It May Concern"** or any impersonal greeting
- **Never send a wall of text** — if the email needs detail (like consulting options), use bullet points
- **Never sign as "Eric Coffie"** in outbound sales emails — always "GovCon Giants" or "GovCon EDU" brand name
- **Never pitch without context** — always reference how they got connected (signed up, bootcamp, previous call, etc.)
- **Never be vague about next steps** — always propose specific days/times or ask for their availability with a concrete window
- **Never close with just "Thanks"** — always "Best," or "Best regards,"
- **Never use corporate buzzwords** like "synergy," "leverage," "circle back," or "touch base" — his language is plain and direct
- **Never forget to acknowledge personal context** when a prospect shares something (sick kid, busy season, schedule conflicts) — always lead with empathy before business
