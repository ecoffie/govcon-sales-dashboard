### Mission
Help small and minority-owned businesses win federal government contracts through training, tools, and consulting. The stated goal is to help 500 companies each achieve $5 million in government contract revenue.

### Founding Story
Eric Coffie was running a construction company when the 2007 real estate crash nearly destroyed his business. A friend with government contracting knowledge showed him how to pivot to federal work, and that saved his company. Between 2008-2012, Eric helped build two 8(a) small businesses from startup to millions in federal contract revenue while simultaneously growing his own firm, Evankoff Construction, from zero to over $20 million in government sales (steel erection, airplane hangars, metal buildings for federal agencies).

After winning a $5 million contract at an Air Force Base in 2017, Eric decided to step back from constant travel and start sharing his knowledge publicly. He felt the federal marketplace was "secretive" -- accessible information was limited to expensive conferences and D.C. trips. He began giving away "99.9% of what he learned for FREE" on YouTube, then launched the podcast in 2019 and built the full platform.

The company is entirely self-funded -- no investors or loans.

### Core Philosophy
"Provide actionable steps (no fluff) that people could use and implement immediately." Eric frames federal contracting as a concrete opportunity, not a product to be sold. The platform leads with free education and earns trust before presenting paid services.

## FAQs

### Getting Started
**Q: Do I need to be a US citizen to get federal contracts?**
A: Not necessarily. Foreign-based companies can participate under certain legal frameworks. GovCon Giants has a free training video specifically on this topic.

**Q: Is SAM.gov registration free?**
A: Yes, completely free. The full validation process takes 3-10 weeks. Never pay someone to register you -- it's free.

**Q: Do I need certifications to win contracts?**
A: No. Certifications (8(a), HUBZone, SDVOSB, WOSB) give you access to set-aside contracts, but there are thousands of full-and-open competitions available to any small business.

**Q: What industries does the government buy from?**
A: Nearly every industry. IT, staffing, construction, consulting, healthcare, janitorial, supplies, manufacturing, professional services, logistics, and hundreds more. If there's a NAICS code for it, the government probably buys it.

**Q: What's the White Glove service?**

**Q: Can my employer pay for training tax-free?**

## Tone & Voice Guide

### Overall Vibe
Warm, direct, professional, peer-to-peer. Eric communicates like a knowledgeable friend giving you a genuine heads-up about an opportunity, not a salesperson pitching a product. The tone is encouraging but realistic -- he won't promise overnight success, but he'll show you the path.

### Key Characteristics
- **Concise:** Most emails are 3-7 sentences. Never walls of text.
- **Empathetic:** Acknowledges busy schedules, life events, missed calls. "No worries at all" is a signature phrase.
- **Action-oriented:** Every communication has a clear next step.
- **Non-pushy persistence:** Will follow up 3-4 times, each with a different angle, always with a graceful exit: "If the timing still isn't right, no worries at all."
- **Opportunity framing:** Federal contracting is positioned as "a reliable new revenue stream" and "a concrete opportunity worth exploring" -- never as a product being sold.
- **Plain language:** Avoids jargon unless contextually necessary. No corporate buzzwords (never "synergy," "leverage," "circle back," "touch base").
- **Data-backed:** Uses specific numbers ($750B+, 23% set-aside, $161B to small businesses) rather than vague claims.

### Phrases Eric Uses Often
- "a reliable new revenue stream"
- "the federal contracting space"
- "worth a 15-minute conversation"
- "walk you through the options"
- "I know things can get busy"
- "no worries at all"
- "help accelerate what you're building"
- "We're here whenever it makes sense"
- "Just 15 minutes is all it takes"
- "Feel free to reach out if you have any questions"
- "Reply YES and I'll get back to you shortly"

### Blog / Content Voice
- Practical, authoritative, yet approachable
- Uses numbered lists and concrete data points
- "Real advice from real contractors" positioning
- Myth-busting framing ("The biggest myth in government contracting? 'You need experience to get experience.'")
- Consistent problem-then-fix structure in educational content
- Direct and prescriptive -- tells you what to do, not just what to consider

### Anti-Patterns (Never Do This)
- Never use "Dear [Name]" or "To Whom It May Concern"
- Never use "I hope this email finds you well"
- Never write long paragraphs or walls of text
- Never be aggressive or guilt-trip ("I've been trying to reach you" energy)
- Never oversell or hype ("AMAZING opportunity," "DON'T MISS OUT")
- Never use multiple exclamation marks
- Never skip the phone number (786-477-0477) in signatures
- Never sign as "Eric Coffie" in outbound sales emails -- always "GovCon Giants" or "GovCon EDU"
- Never pitch without referencing context (how they signed up, previous call, bootcamp, etc.)
- Never use corporate buzzwords
- Never close with just "Thanks" -- always "Best," or "Best regards,"
